Year In Review: 2024

2024-12-31

2024 is now in the books!

In prior years for work I would write an internal post about the year to help us realize all of the things we managed to accomplish that year. I found the research and reflection while writing the post to be very revealing. We some times get so caught up in the daily grind, we aren’t able to see what we’ve managed to accomplish. This year going to give it a try applying it to my personal life.

Here’s a TL;DR of the year:

Personal Life

  • A year free from Caffiene
  • Started back into Martial Arts
  • Started Working Out more
  • Lost 12 pounds
  • Traveled to Brazil and London
  • Experienced some culture with Hamilton and Star Wars at the Houston Symphony

Professionally

  • Focused on Production Readiness
  • Focused on being a better Staff Engineer
  • Changed jobs after almost 10 years at the same company
  • Got to be hands on with some new technology
  • Stuck with single desktop setup for most of the year

Personal Life

As with any year.. Full of ups and downs. But overall I feel like I made some good progress in a few areas.

Health

Improving my health has been a big focus for me this year. Thankfully this isn’t because i’ve started having any serious problems. But I have come to realize its only going to get harder to get healthly the older I get.

A Year Free From Caffiene

Back in October of 2023. I came to realization that I could not function without caffeine. Dr. Pepper to be precise. If we ran out in the house and I had a busy day and was unable to go get more.. I would suffer the whole day. This felt like addiction to me.

I made the decision to cut it completely and haven’t had a Dr. Pepper or any other caffinated soda since then. As of October this year that was a whole year without it.

The downside is I replaced that with other sodas that were high in sugar. So while I was free from caffiene.. I wasn’t really seeing any health benefits from this change.

A few months ago I started conciously drinking less soda and focus on reducing the sugar intake.

Martial Arts

I’ve always been a big fan of Martial Arts. Even earned my Yellow Belt in Karate back in college. But stopped at the time because I was a cheap college kid that couldn’t at the time justify the cost of class.

My kids have all been in Karate all year. Decided to join them. So i’m back up to yellow belt and intend to carry on as its become a great way to blow off steam and disconnect.

Working out

I’ve slowly been building out a home gym and regularly doing extra exercise. I hope to continue to increase this into 2025.

Impact

I feel a lot better. I’ve lost 12 pounds since I started this journey only 4 months ago.

I can now spar without feeling like i’m going to pass out.

Travel

Took a work trip to Brazil. Crazy thing is I went through the airport in Port Alegre only a week before the entire airport was flooded.

Took my wife and kids to London. This was my kids first time out of country.

Culture

I’ve been trying experience a bit of “culture” in recent years.

I saw Hamilton performed in London this year. It was a lot more moving in person. Also I don’t get why its so much cheaper to see plays in London than anywhere here in the US…

I also got to experience Star Wars at the Houston Symphony this year. I’d recommend.

Work / Professional

2024 involved a rather big change for my professionally.

Production Readiness

This is something I spent a good bit of time on during the first part of the year. Taking existing services and focusing on establishing SLA/SLOs. Identifying what is important for the user experience. Then working backwards to identify what risks making delivering this possible.

While not the first time thinking about this. It was really a time of formalizing things and making architectural changes in order to make it happen.

Staff Engineer

Over the course of my career at Rocket.Chat i’ve held a number of roles. Everything from just core contributor, to Engineering Manager, to Tech Lead, to Team Lead and a bunch of things in between.

This year I spent some time trying to settle into the role of Staff Engineer / Architect with out any of the management components.

I’ve found that I really like the role of staff engineer. Its a good mixture of coding, writing, leading (not managing), mentoring and helping aligning across teams.

New Job

One of the biggest changes for me this year was changing jobs. I had been at Rocket.Chat for almost 10 years. The original team I built spun off into 3 seperate teams. The downside to this is I still was heavily involved in all 3 of them. In retrospect I was acting a bit like a director setting roadmaps and direction of the teams, while being inside one of the teams, and being a Staff Engineer / Architect. My work/life balance was off and I was struggling to reset it. Even though I’d take time off.. I was unable to fully disconnect.

I got a job offer to be a Senior Engineer at a company called Underline who is building fiber networks in communities and all of the software it takes to make that happen. I’ve always been interested in global infrastructure and how they are built and operate. So I took the job as a chance to shake things up, reset and learn something new. Part of me was also curious what it would be like to be just a software engineer again.

I’ve quickly come to realize that i’m not ok just being a software engineer. I don’t enjoy just taking other peoples plans and ideas and just executing them. I enjoy being involved in the architecture and direction of the software. I’m not sure if this is just because i’m used to it or if its just how I am wired.

Technology

Part of the new job means working with some new technology.

Microservices

I had previously worked with microservices. But typically only in the context of breaking off a piece of a monolith in order to enable scale. Never in a microservice first architecture.

The downside of microservices is the complexity they bring. I can imagine very large teams microservices could potentially enable not just scale in the load, but also allow multiple teams to work on different parts of a system in parallel.

In a small team and when scale for load isn’t needed.. i’m still not convinced its the right choice. But i’m open to being convinced.

Graphql

I had previously tried graphql. Mostly consuming it. It always seemed like the idea was cool. That you could make less api calls and get all of the information you needed.

In practice… I don’t think it really reduces the number of api calls. It also means more calls bouncing around inside the network to get the data. Making authorization harder cause you’ve got to make sure the user is authorized to fetch each piece of information instead of just authorized to make the call.

Also it leads to a lot of boilerplate code. I still prefer a RESTful api.

GRPC and GRPC Gateway

I’ve watched and read about GRPC and Protobufs for a while. But never had a good reason to use them.

Being able to define a protobuf service and from that generate the types as well as a full swagger documentation for a rest api and grpc-gateway code is pretty cool.

I’ll definitely be playing with this more in the future.

Daily Tech Stack

For all of 2024 i’ve used Silverblue. An Ostree/Immutable Operating system. All desktop apps are installed via flatpaks. Other programs are layered on top, or installed in a dev container. I’ve got a custom ostree branch called Triiodide (triiodide is 3 iodine atoms - i).

i3wm is still my window manager of choice. Having applications assigned to certain workspaces and certain workspaces assigned to certain monitors is the optimization i’ve made this year.

Some day i’ll make a post about this.

A new addition is Ghostty. Previously I used alacritty. Nothing wrong with it. But i’m a fan of the creator of Ghostty and I like the focus on performance and simplicity.

Also spent the year with a Single 32” Curved Monitor with a secondary small dedicated monitor for chat, music and video calls.

I really like this simple setup.

2025

I’m looking forward to 2025. I plan to continue to focus on my health. I also hope to get back to writing more as well as back to doing more Staff Engineer / Architect type work.
